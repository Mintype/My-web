My 6th Grade Experience - Last Updated June 30, 2019

My 1st day of 6th grade at Woodrow Wilson Middle School was September 7th, 2019. When I first came, I was

already 3 days late. When I arrived, the school seemed small to me. But, when I got inside, it was like a mansion. I wasn’t expecting it to be this big. Of course it was a shocker to me to see it. The first day, my classes were all over the place. Upstairs, downstairs, upstairs and downstairs, etc. The classes that I had downstairs were confusing because the classes were in areas where barely anybody went. But, I still got to my classes. The rest of the year I found my classes much more easily.

The first marking period, I had a lot of missing assignments in math because there used to be so much

homework that I did not know what was homework and what wasn’t. Then later on I found where to find my homework. Mrs. Marrocco had posted it in my google classroom. It was called “MarroccoMath” Homework Calendar 2018-19. Because of that I rarely got any missing assignments. In my english class, I had a classmate kept talking about a teacher which threw most of the people off task. It was really annoying when you kept getting irritated. I could barely focus because of that.

In 6th grade I had a couple of friends. Can you guess how many friends I have? I have about 10 friends that I talk

to a lot. 10 is better than nothing. Some of these friends moved, Having someone to talk to didn’t make me feel lonely. It felt as if I had some company. Most of them were in a lot of my classes. Having some friends is good and doesn’t make you feel as if you are the only person in the school.

The second marking period went a little better than the first because I got better grades than the first marking

period. My grades were either B+s, A- or A+s. They increased my overall grade. This was one of the best grades that I have ever gotten in my school life. This marking period, I joined the Indian Cultural Club. In this club, we made food and made things to celebrate Indian festivals such as Holi, Gudi Padwa, Diwali. We even celebrated things for Christmas and Halloween. In Social Studies, we learned about Egypt and India. It was actually fun. We learned new things while having fun. In Science, we were learning about fossils and different types of rocks. Since I had already learned about it, it wasn’t as exciting as the S.T.E.M. project.

The third and Fourth Marking Period were the best! I got very high grades. By this time I had a lot of friends and

knew exactly where my classes were. I also started health in the fourth marking period. Because of health, I missed out on the fitness days where I had to run 8 laps around the field in a limited time. That really tired me out.

Every grade in Woodrow Wilson has a field trip. But this year 6th grade didn’t have it because there were too

many students. But in exchange for a field trip we had a field day. Now that made some of the 7thand 8th graders jealous. That was actually some good news to hear.

The last day of school was really OK. We only watched movies and during Health we went to the gym and all

we did was sit there and talk for the entire class. In English we watched Middle School: The Worst Years of my life. In Social Studies, we watched Percy Jackson: The Lightning Thief. In french, we watched Hugo. In Band, we watched Wonder. In Science, we watched Bedtime Stories. My favorite movie was Wonder. It was my favorite movie because it actually expresses what happens in Middle Schools.

Keep Calm and Move On - Last Updated July 2, 2019

If you have a favorite thing and your parents just come in and take it away, how do you feel? Angry? Mad? Disappointed? Well for those of you

who do not know what I am saying, here is an example.

Let’s say you are told that you can do whatever you want for an hour and you choose to play Pub-G,Fortnite, Minecraft, etc. but your parents

barge in and take the game away from you and don’t even get to save your progress, how do you feel? Try to remember if this ever happened to you before. I’ll wait.

--------------

Already back? Did you remember? If this didn’t happen to you yet, just hang on, it will happen to you one day. Okay, so let’s get to the story.

That’s what happened to me on the night of June, 2019. My parents went to watch a movie on their projector in their room when I felt as if it

were unfair even though I watched a movie in the afternoon..

So this is what happened: My parents went to watch a movie on their projector in their room and expected me to sleep. So instead, I just started

reading the book, Percy Jackson: The Last Olympian in the dark, which I am not allowed to do at night and sleep on time. When my parents finished their movie and came out, they saw me reading a book. They didn’t react. I kept my book right beside me. When they turned on a light, I started reading again in hopes that they won’t get mad at me for reading in the light but instead my mom started scolding me.

“We told you to close the book!” she said. She stomped over and closed the book. The thing that most angered me wasn’t her closing the book,

it was her not even looking at the page number. Not looking at the page number is like not saving your progress on a certain video game. So, in return, I kicked the object she was holding.

[Do not do this! It is rude!]

I wasn’t even aware that my dad was looking. When he saw this he quickly came over to me and told me to do a stand-up and sit-down type of

punishment. In this punishment, you have to do squats with you holding your earlobes. But you have to go all the way down. Even doing 50 tires me out a lot and I was expected to do 300 of those.

Anyway, even if you rage, you won’t get all of your progress back. So, just leave it alone.

If there was something I learned from this experience it would be to calm yourself down and then tell the person what they should have done.

By doing this, I won’t have to say sorry and do a punishment, but instead my mom would have apologized to me. Now that I am writing this, I feel sorry for what I did.

Follow these steps if you don’t want to get in trouble:


1. Count to 10 or 20


2. Calmly tell the person what you would have done


3. If they don’t listen to you, well if it’s a book, then you can find the event you were on. If it’s a video game, then you got an advantage. You can now come to the spot where you were even quicker.


*Moral*: Stay Calm and Move on.
